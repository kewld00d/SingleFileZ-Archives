Section 1 - Setup This is honestly one of the most important sections to this guide. I can not stress enough how much the different things I'm going to describe here will effect the way your funk smokes. A) Your pipe. - Personally, I use a blue dot "oil burner". I do this because the glass is much thicker hence more durable (and I've dropped that thing more times then you could possibly imagine), and also because I can control the heat better which is also an effect of having the thicker glass. I also tend to use a larger pipe than most people. To give you an idea, my bowl is roughly the size of a ping-pong ball. B) If you already have a pipe, make sure that it's clean. - The best way that I've found to just at least make sure that the bowl is clean enough that you can drop some funk in there and smoke it without getting anything burnt mixed in, is if you take a torch lighter, turn the flame adjustment all the way up, and then put the flame on the bowl to where the inner of the two flames the torch produces is about 1/8 of an inch away from the glass. Then just move the flame around the bowl and torch all spots on it. You may see the old funk turn bright red and get scared thinking it's gonna blow up in your hand, but I assure you that if you hold the heat on it for a second or two after it turns red, it will just disintegrate. Do this until the glass is pretty uniformly clean and then let it cool. Keep in mind that after doing this your glass is going to be insanely hot, so make sure you either set it on something that's not going to burn (gently so you don't dent the glass since it's hot), or plan on holding it for a while. This cleaning method can be done on pipes that have thinner glass, but I would not hold the flame as close and pay very close attention to what I'm doing. (I would also very much like to stress that it's incredibly important that you fully clean your pipe every time you load another bowl, no matter how dirty it is or not. I have had many a bowl not go the way I wanted simply because there was just a little bit of brown in there. Don't be lazy, just clean it.) C) Your heat source. - I will always recommend a torch lighter all day, every day. Some might call me crazy for this, and that's understandable because if you don't know how to smoke whilst operating a torch, you're gonna have a bad time. The key elements are how you use it (I'll get into that in the actual smoking section), as well as the way the torch itself is made (more particularly what it's flame is like). My personal favorite torch of mine to use, is a very basic, single flame, Eagle Torch. It's very durable, can get very hot and be basically unaffected, and it has flame that is slightly wider in diameter than most of the other pocket sized single flames I see. This is good because using my method, you will be able to not only have more heat dispersion across your glass, but you'll also be able to focus more on actually using the heat from the flame as apposed to the flame itself. D) You will obviously need some Funk. - Once you have all of these things, proceed to the next section. Section 2 - Smoking Here I will go through all of the steps that I perform when loading a new bowl as well as taking a hit, in order. Step 1) Loading the bowl. - There are many mixed opinions on how much Funk to throw in a bowl. Personally, I usually throw in anywhere between .4 and a half gram. That seems like a lot to most, but keep in mind I will have that one bowl going and be gacked for about a day and a half. That is without burning it, and without worrying about having to clean my pipe again and loading up another bowl. I find that when I load more in, I get much larger hits, and I get much higher. Your funk will also probably do it's first meltdown better if you try to put similar sized rocks in there as well. Although having similar rocks at one time isn't the most crucial thing in the world, it definitely doesn't hurt. Step 2) Melting it down. - Now that we're getting into the part about using the torch, I want to say that I never touch either the outside or the inside flame to the glass while there's funk in there, and I usually have the adjustment set so that the tip of the outer flame is about an inch away from the base of the inner flame. Once you have your Funk in the bowl, go ahead and start circling the perimeter of the little pile of it slowly, with the tip of the outer blue flame about an inch or an inch and half away. It will take some time to melt down, although if you feel that you're holding it so far that some of the Funk is recrystallizing, feel free to move slightly closer. Just maintain as much distance as you can. Once it starts smoking, you should have a pretty decent puddle rolling. Twist your pipe side to side and breathe in from the mouth piece slowly. Continue to spin, breathe, and heat just until all of the crystals are melted down all the way. Once that happens take it off of the heat and slowly roll the pipe side to side while the Funk in there recrystallizes, letting it go about as far up each side of the bowl to make a sideways parenthesis symbol, or if you looked at it like a clock, about 4 and 8 at most. Step 3) Taking a hit. (I would like to ask that especially in this section, you make sure to have a good understanding of what to do when according to these steps when trying my method. As I said before, to the untrained operator using a torch can potentially not go very well in the burning department if you mess up too bad. And nobody wants that ) - Alright. At this point we've gone over a lot of basics, and some not so difficult things that don't take too long to master, but now it's time for the real deal. In an attempt to make this step as easy to read/follow along with as possible, I'm going to create another series of steps that are more detailed, with a "bullet points" version following that you can (hopefully) follow along with as you smoke, if you'd like to. Step 3.1 - Light your torch and assure the flame is adjusted accordingly to how I described in "Step 2" Step 3.2 - Place the flame at about the same distance as seen in "Step 2) Melting it down." and again, slowly trace the perimeter of your, what is right now, crystallized puddle. While doing this, be sure to keep the flame the same distance away from the glass the entire time you're "outlining" the puddle. An easy way to do this is to hold the torch so the flame is pointing straight up in the air and move the pipe as apposed to the former. (Pro-Tip #1 - I actually put the whole pipe above my head while I do this so I can see things better) Step 3.3 - Right now you should be holding your flame around your bowl and be making it trace the perimeter of your puddle in some way or another. If you haven't already, you will start to see the bottom "layer" of your puddle melt. Continue to outline the puddle until the Funk liquefies enough to start moving back and forth across he bowl if you were to twist the pipe side to side. This will probably be when about half of your Funky puddle is melted down. What to do with the funk when it gets to this point is where most people and I differ in opinion. But trust me, if you perfect this part, you will be satisfied with your hit, every time. Step 3.4 - So your puddle just started rolling, and you're starting to see some smoke. Even though it's awfully tempting, wait just a tad more to take any of it in. Instead, use this time that the bowl isn't absolutely filled with smoke (vision is key!) as an opportunity to make sure that the puddle is sitting nicely right in the middle of the bowl, directly below the carb hole on top, and remember that at this point you should still be outlining the puddle. Step 3.5 - The puddle is mostly if not all liquid by now, is smoking a little, and you're probably starting to get a little bit concerned about burning your Funk, aren't you? Don't worry, your Funk is gonna be just fine, you just need to be vigilant about how your puddle is acting and watch for the signs I described at the beginning of this step. If you're here, then take the flame completely away from the bowl, exhale the majority of your breath outside of the pipe while using the tail end of your exhale to blow through pipe which will cause the puddle to start vaporizing like crazy. You only want to exhale for about 1/2 to 1 second. At that point you'll start inhaling which I'll talk about a little in the next step. Step 3.6 - The first important thing to remember about this step is that all the while you're inhaling, you need to twist the pipe back and forth. Make an effort to keep the pipe horizontal while you're doing this, I've found it to produce the best results (Although you will more than likely find that your natural tendency is to hold it at a slight downward angle, and this is fine. The point is less to hold it completely level, and more about making sure that you stay conscious of not holding it at too much of a downward angle, as this will make it so that your Funk does not roll around the bowl correctly). You'll want to twist the pipe about the same amount as in "Step 1". When you inhale, you need to do it very slowly and gently. I'm not saying to go as slow as is humanly possible for you, but don't hit it like you're trying to smoke pretty much anything else one might smoke. Take a deep breath as if you're stressed and trying to control your breathing to calm down. As you're inhaling you'll see all the smoke coming off of the puddle that you're taking in. But soon, the amount of smoke being produced will start to decrease more and more, so then what do you do? Well at this point you have 2 options. You can either decide that you've already had a big enough hit and you don't think you can take much more (happens to me a lot at the beginning of one of my phat bowls ), or you can make that Funk start smoking again. If you choose the former, congratulations, you just took a hit using my method and I hope it worked great! If you go with the latter, then please read on to the next step. Step 3.7 - In order to make the puddle start smoking again mid hit, firstly, you have to start heating it again at the right time. In my experience, I would say that this is right around when you see that your smoke production has decreased by give or take about half of when you started to inhale. Once you've noticed those signs (again, vision is key!), go ahead and move your torch flame back under the puddle. Except this time you're gonna keep the tip of the flame anywhere from a quarter inch (if not, ever so slightly closer but again not touching the flame to the glass) to about a half of an inch away. As your flame is under the puddle make sure to continue spinning the bowl at a slower rate than before, and while following your Funky puddle with said flame, move flame back and forth in small circular motions in order to cover the entire area (not just perimeter this time) of your puddle. This is performed as a very quick motion, and you should only keep the flame on there just until your smoke starts going the way you like again. At that point just rinse and repeat taking the flame on and off as I just explained until, A. You run out of breath and need to let your hit out. Or B. You're starting to burn your Funk. The two main causes of burning that Funky Goodness when using this method, are how long/much heat you're putting into that Funk when you're re-applying your flame to continue your hit midway, and how long it's taking you overall to complete your hit. This is because the more times that you reapply heat to your puddle, your bowl in it's entirety heats up. This is detrimental because Funk smokes most when it is cooling down, not heating up (this is why I focus intently on heating the puddle until it's moving and then some. It packs heat in the spot where the puddle is, but then when the puddle is moved away from that heat, it starts to smoke like a volcano). So again, be vigilant of how your puddle is acting. If it seems like even though you apply more heat in what looks like/is the correct spot, chances are that the entire glass bowl is just too hot overall for the Funk to cool down the way you need it to. At that point, you can either wait for the Funk to recrystallize or, if you're feeling ballsy enough, grab a slightly damp rag (wet, but dry enough so that no water comes out when you squeeze it) and gently run it across outer surface of the bowl to achieve instant cool down. (Be careful using the rag, as there is a pretty good chance that you're pipe could shatter on you from changing temperature so rapidly. Just please, be careful) And lastly for this step will be (Pro-Tip #2 - Take a second to identify which hand you prefer to hold your torch with when smoking.
